What about traffic and parking?

Traffic safety is a street-design problem. Parking is a cost we've been mandating.

The 30-second answer

Density typically means more people who can walk or bike to destinations and fewer car trips per household. The Village's Vision Zero work is the better answer to traffic than freezing the population, because injuries are a function of street design, not car count. On parking: minimums add cost without serving any goal worth having — they make housing more expensive, encourage car dependency, and consume land that could be homes. Let demand and design set counts; permit zones handle spillover where on-street parking is tight.
