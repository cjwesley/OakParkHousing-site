The case for reform

Fiscal sustainability.draft More households sharing a capped levy, and retail districts that generate uncapped sales tax. Nº19 Why reform? Stay in your neighborhood at every stage of life.draft Sell the 4BR, move to a ground-floor unit on your own block — and keep paying into the schools that raised your kids. Nº20 Why reform? The questions

Nº1 New construction is all luxury. How does that help affordability?ready Oak Park built 50 affordable units. It gained 1,350. The market did the rest. Nº2 Isn't 'filtering' just trickle-down economics for housing?ready Trickle-down was a tax theory that failed. Filtering is a used-car market that works. Nº3 Didn't the affordable gains come from the IHO, Van Buren, and the Housing Authority?ready Every mandated unit is already inside the 50. The other 1,300 remain unexplained — except by filtering. Nº4 Why not just strengthen the inclusionary housing ordinance instead?ready The IHO only fires when something gets built. 20% of zero is zero. Nº5 Won't this cause gentrification and displacement?ready Displacement is caused by demand hitting a supply that's forbidden to grow. Nº6 What about traffic and parking?ready Traffic safety is a street-design problem. Parking is a cost we've been mandating. Nº7 Won't new housing overwhelm our schools?draft The households new housing attracts pay into schools they barely use. That's a subsidy to school families. Nº8 Can our sewers and infrastructure handle more housing?draft Infrastructure objections are engineering questions with engineering answers — and they're rarely asked in good faith. Nº9 What about neighborhood character?draft There's a character that's about beauty and walkability, and one that's about who gets to live here. I'll protect the first and vote against the second. Nº10 Will this hurt my property value?draft The Hinsdale comparison is backwards — exclusionary zoning preserving value by pricing people out is the model being defended, not avoided. Nº11 Aren't we already diverse? Half of Oak Park is apartments.draft Diverse at the village level, sorted at the structural level — by tenure and housing type instead of explicit lines. Nº12 Why not put it to a referendum and let the people decide?draft Every zoning change in Oak Park's history was Board action. 'Let the people decide' has a history — and it lost. Nº13 The racist history is real, but what does it have to do with today's zoning?draft Single-family zoning isn't different from the racial exclusion it replaced. It's derived from it. Nº14 Can't we just allow ADUs and coach houses and stop there?draft We already allow ADUs — unless you live in the third of the village where the historic district quietly cancels them. Nº15 Why now? This feels rushed.draft We've been working on this for years. This is what informing the community looks like. Nº16 Will this raise my property taxes?draft The levy is capped by state law. More households divide it; they don't add to it.
